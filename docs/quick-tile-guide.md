# Quick Settings Tile Guide

Use this guide after opening **Quick Settings Tile > Config** inside DhwaniControl.


## Step 1: Open Quick Settings

1. Swipe down from the top of the screen once.
2. Swipe down again to fully expand Quick Settings.
3. Confirm you can see all tiles and the edit control.

![Open Quick Settings](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/RajnishKMehta/images/refs/heads/main/DhwaniControl/quick-tile-step-1.webp)

## Step 2: Enter Edit Mode

1. Tap the edit control (usually a pencil icon or **Edit tiles** button).
2. Scroll Down or swipe left to available tiles (buttons).
3. Find **$\textcolor{#D00000}{\textsf{Volume Panel}}$** from *DhwaniControl* in the available tiles section.

![Enter Edit Mode](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/RajnishKMehta/images/refs/heads/main/DhwaniControl/quick-tile-step-2.webp)

## Step 3: Add the Volume Panel Tile

1. Drag it into your active tiles list.
2. Place it where it is easy to reach.

![Add the Volume Panel Tile](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/RajnishKMehta/images/refs/heads/main/DhwaniControl/quick-tile-step-3.webp)

## Step 4: Save and Test

1. Save the tile layout and exit edit mode.
> Note:
> On certain devices, settings are saved automatically when navigating back. If no save button is visible, simply go back.
2. Open Quick Settings again.
3. Tap **$\textcolor{#52ff80}{\textsf{Volume Panel}}$** and confirm Android volume controls open.

![Save and Test](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/RajnishKMehta/images/refs/heads/main/DhwaniControl/quick-tile-step-4.webp)

## Quick Troubleshooting

- Tile not visible: reopen edit mode and check available tiles again.
- Tile tap does nothing: reopen DhwaniControl once, then test again.
- OEM skin differences: labels and layout may vary by device brand.
